The formula:

$$ P(candidate\ wins\ presidency)/P(candidate\ wins\ nomination) $$

Comes from the conditional probability formula:

$$ P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} $$

Thus, a clear assumption here is that winning the presidency must mean winning the party nomination (because of the 'and' in the numerator). This means that my model ignores the possibility of a third party or independent winning. This probably drags the democrats ever so slightly above where they should be.
